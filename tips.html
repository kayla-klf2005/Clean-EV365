Finally, here are our top ten tips and prevention strategies that we as citzens can use to fight Imporper Waste Disposal:

    1. Buy only the quantity of the hazardous product that you need to use

    -By purchasing only what you can immediately use and using ALL of the product according to the manufacturer's directions, you will not have to worry about disposing of unused waste.

    2. Buy the least toxic product that will do the job.

    - Many jobs can be done with less or nontoxic products. For example, vinegar can be used as a nontoxic household cleaner. A list of other alternatives is available anywhere online.

    3. Educate yourself on Hazardous Waste Management

    - Educate yoursel and practice proper hazardous waste disposal. Find out what are the proper steps for extraction of hazardous waste in your community from your local Waste Disposal Company. However, avoid disposing of hazardous waste material until the product is finished or entirely used.

    4. Take reusable bags to the store when shopping

    -By using reusable bags when shopping, you avoid having excess plastic bags to throw away after its use at home. This would result in less plastic reaching our landfills.

    5. Reduce or eliminate the use of non-biodegradable paper plates and cups

    -Although proven very useful in cases of parties and events where you would want cheap cutlery and plates for one use only, all these non-biodegradable items would end up being thrown away, where it would eventually make its way to a landfill and slowly deteriorate.

    6. Store leftover foods in reusable containers instead of single use plastic bags or Polystyrene foam containers

    -Instead of using single use containers, reusable containers give you a longer lifespan in terms of strength of the container and it is better for storage in enclosed spaces.

    7. Donate unwanted, slightly used clothing, furniture and other household items to local non-profit organizations

    -Instead of throwing away your old clothes, furniture and household items, give them away or give them to charity! There is always someone in need of these things somewhere in your country or even worldwide!

    8. Take advantage of the many drop-off recycling opportunities

    -SWMCOL(Solid Waste Management Company Limited), in partnership with iCare, have come together to create recycling drop-off points at certain locations in the country. Persons are asked to place their recyclables in see-through plastic bags according to the recyclable categories (Plastics, aluminum, cardboard boxes, and electronic wastes) and drop it off in the bin closest to you. These bins are then emptied and taken for recycling See the map here for the closest bin to you!

    9.Utilise Composting Methods

    -Composting is defined as a mixture that consists largely of decayed organic matter and is used for fertilizing and conditioning land. Using food scraps has been proven extremely useful for composting. So instead of throwing away food scraps, create a compost pile! You would get compost to help enrich soil for your plants and you avoid having unnecessary waste reach the landfills.

    10. Avoid purchasing foods with pre-packaging in containers and single-use plastic

    -By purchasing food in bulks or one with very minimal packaging, you reduce the amount of waste (packaging) that reaches the landfill.
